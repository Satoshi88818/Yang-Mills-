## Abstract

The sectional curvature of the orbit space M = 𝒜/𝒢 diverges positively as configurations approach the Gribov horizon ∂Ω (where λ₀ → 0). However, a mass gap requires curvature bounded **away from zero everywhere inside** the Gribov region Ω — even in classically weak-field regimes.

Raw Ricci curvature may dip low in such regions, but the physically relevant geometry is weighted by a horizon-repulsive measure. This introduces a drift → the **Bakry-Émery Ricci tensor** Ric_BE = Ric + Hess f (with f encoding horizon penalty) can remain uniformly positive.

This document outlines a concrete, step-by-step programme to prove  
**inf_{A ∈ Ω} Ric_BE(A) ≥ Δ_BE(g,N) > 0**  
uniformly, using weighted comparison geometry, Bochner identities, and curvature-dimension conditions in infinite dimensions.

## 1. Why Raw Curvature Is Insufficient – The Weighted Picture

- In principal stratum, raw sectional curvatures are positive near horizon but can approach 0 in weak-coupling directions (small ||A||, almost abelian).
- Physical path integral restricts to Ω + horizon functional → effective measure μ ≈ exp(-f) vol_g, where f penalizes proximity to ∂Ω (e.g., f ~ dist² or Tr(M_A^{-p})).
- The relevant curvature operator becomes the **weighted/Bakry-Émery Ricci tensor**:

  Ric_BE(f) = Ric_g + Hess_g f

- Modern theorems (Bakry-Émery–Lichnerowicz, Bakry-Ledoux) extend spectral gap bounds to weighted manifolds if Ric_BE ≥ K > 0 and CD(K, ∞) holds → goal is to prove K = Δ_BE > 0 uniformly.

## 2. Choice of Weight Function f (Horizon Regularization)

Promising candidates (regularized to avoid singularities):

1. f(A) = α ∫ Tr( (M_A^{-1})^p ) d⁴x   (p small, α tuned)
2. f(A) ≈ β ⋅ dist_M(A, ∂Ω)^2   (geodesic distance proxy via 1/√λ₀ or horizon functional)
3. f(A) = γ ⋅ sup_{x} Tr( ghost propagator(x,x) )   (Kugo-Ojima inspired)

Goal properties:
- f → +∞ as λ₀ → 0 (repulsion from horizon)
- Hess f ≥ c > 0 near boundary + bounded/positive deep inside
- f smooth enough for Hessian & Bochner formulas

## 3. Step-by-Step Programme

### Step 1: Local Positivity Around Classical Minima
- Take basepoint A₀ with ||A₀||_{L²} ≪ 1 (almost flat, λ₀ large).
- Linearize M_A ≈ -Δ (abelian), but non-abelian commutators + global topology induce small positive Hess f.
- Compute Hess f explicitly via perturbation:
  - Variation δA → δλ₀ ≈ quadratic form in commutators
  - Hess f ≥ ε(local) > 0 from mixing of zero modes / instanton-like fluctuations
- Prove Ric_BE ≥ ε > 0 in geodesic balls B_r(A₀) (small r).

### Step 2: Exploit Horizon as Positivity Barrier
- Already proven: K_M → +∞ as dist(A, ∂Ω) → 0 (∼ 1/λ₀).
- Use this to bound Hess f from below near boundary.
- Apply barrier / comparison principle:
  - Consider distance function d(A) = inf geodesic length to ∂Ω
  - Show Hess d² ≥ positive constant (convexity-like) in collar neighborhood of ∂Ω
  - Patch with interior regions via maximum principle on Ric_BE along minimizing geodesics

### Step 3: Global Control via Bochner & Weitzenböck Identities
- Derive weighted Bochner formula for the Bakry-Emery Laplacian Δ_f = Δ_g + ∇f · ∇:
  ```
  (1/2) Δ_f |∇u|² = |Hess u|² + ⟨∇u, ∇ Δ_f u⟩ + Ric_BE(∇u, ∇u)
  ```
- Integrate over Ω (with decay at infinity) → if boundary terms controlled by horizon blow-up, Ric_BE cannot dip too negative without contradicting positivity near ∂Ω.
- Use integrated curvature identities (analogous to positive mass theorem) to bound inf Ric_BE.

### Step 4: Curvature-Dimension Condition CD(Δ_BE, ∞)
- Verify (or approximate) CD(K, ∞) for (M, g, μ = e^{-f} vol_g):
  - Displacement convexity of relative entropy Ent_μ along L²-Wasserstein geodesics
- If CD(Δ_BE, ∞) holds with Δ_BE > 0, then:
  - Log-Sobolev inequality ⇒ exponential decay of heat semigroup
  - Spectral gap ≥ Δ_BE / 2 for -Δ_f
- This projects to mass gap in quantized theory (via Gribov-Zwanziger or equivalent).

### Step 5: Asymptotics at Spatial Infinity
- In ℝ⁴, require decay (Sobolev embedding, L²-based spaces).
- Show Ric_BE → 0 at infinity is incompatible:
  - Use monotonicity along projected YM gradient flow
  - Or contradiction via integrated curvature / positive mass analogs
- Goal: floor on Ric_BE from large-distance behavior.

### Step 6: Validation & Partial Results Roadmap
- **Lower dimensions first:** Prove uniform Ric_BE > 0 in 2+1D YM (known mass gap).
- **Lattice proxy:** On large tori, compute discrete orbit-space Ric_BE → check infimum & scaling.
- **Toy models:** SU(2) on T³ × ℝ, Kaluza-Klein reductions.
- **Falsifiability:** Seek sequence A_n ∈ Ω with Ric_BE(A_n) → 0 → must hit horizon or infinity → contradict volume finiteness or barrier.
